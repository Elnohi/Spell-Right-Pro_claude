How SpellRightPro works, and why listen-and-type beats flashcards

Most spelling tools test recognition: they show you a word and ask if it looks right. That's a different skill from the one you need under exam pressure, in a classroom, or live at a spelling bee — producing the word correctly from memory, with nobody showing you the answer first. SpellRightPro trains production: you hear the word, you type it, you get instant feedback. That is exactly how the OET writing sub-test works.

The app covers three audiences with three separate word banks:

OET medical spelling — 1,511 healthcare terms across drug names, anatomical vocabulary, clinical abbreviations, and symptom descriptors. Words are randomised using Fisher-Yates shuffle so you never practise the same order twice. You can choose British, American, or Australian accent (Premium), run a timed 24-word exam simulation, or work through the full list with session save so you pick up exactly where you stopped. Our OET spelling guide lists the most commonly misspelled medical words if you want to start there.

School spelling practice — 307 curriculum-aligned words covering primary through secondary level, plus the ability to upload your own classroom list in seconds. Real-time marking highlights errors character by character so students see exactly where they went wrong, not just a pass/fail.

Spelling Bee mode — 500+ competition words with voice recognition that listens to you spell letter-by-letter, the way a real competition judge does, rather than just typing into a box. Adaptive difficulty automatically adjusts which words you see based on your recent accuracy.

SpellRightPro was built by a practising doctor who prepared for OET and found no tool that combined a medical word bank, audio playback, and production-based practice in one place. Frequently Asked Questions

Is SpellRightPro free?

Yes. All three practice modes — OET, School, and Spelling Bee — are completely free with no sign-up required. Premium plans add unlimited custom lists, all accent options, adaptive drills, and detailed progress analytics.

Does it cover the OET spelling word list?

Yes. The OET module contains 1,511 medical words covering drug names, anatomical terms, clinical abbreviations, and symptom descriptors — the vocabulary that most commonly appears in OET writing tasks. It was assembled by a practising doctor who used it to prepare for their own OET exam.

Can I use SpellRightPro for Spelling Bee competition practice?

Yes. Spelling Bee mode uses voice recognition so you spell each word aloud, letter by letter, just as you would in a real competition. It includes 500+ competition-level words with adaptive difficulty that increases as your accuracy improves.

How is SpellRightPro different from other spelling apps?

Most spelling apps test recognition — they show you a word and ask if it looks right. SpellRightPro tests production: you hear the word, you type it from memory, and you get character-level feedback on exactly what you got wrong. It also has a dedicated OET medical word bank, which most general spelling tools don’t offer.

Does it work on Android and iPhone?

Yes. SpellRightPro is a Progressive Web App (PWA) that works in any modern browser on any device. It is also available as a native Android app on Google Play. An iOS app is planned.

Can I upload my own word list?

Yes. In School mode you can paste or upload a custom word list and practise it immediately. Free users can save one custom list per day; Premium users can save unlimited lists.
